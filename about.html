1. How many species of birds can fly?


        There are about 10,000 living species of birds, almost all of which can fly.
         Flying birds are divided into 29 orders. The most common orders include landfowl, 
            waterfowl, shorebirds, diurnal and nocturnal raptors, parrots, and perching birds.
        2. Why can't kakapos fly?



            Kakapos: The Flightless Parrot You've Never Heard Off - C.S.W.D
            Kakapo are missing a part of their body that other flying birds
            have called the keel. The keep is attached to the sternum and 
            works directly with the flight muscles, as they normally attach 
            to the keel. They also have very little wings. So, Kakapo are flightless.
